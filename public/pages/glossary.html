3
3DA Stats
Three Dart Average - The average number of points scored per 3 darts (one turn) in X01 games. A key measure of overall scoring ability. Higher is better.
Example: A 3DA of 60 means scoring 60 points per turn on average
B
BUST Gameplay
When a player's score goes below zero OR lands on exactly 1 in X01 games. The turn is voided and the score reverts to what it was before that turn. Also called "busting" or "going bust."
Example: With 32 remaining, throwing 33 or more causes a bust
C
CHECKOUT Gameplay
Successfully finishing an X01 game by hitting the exact remaining score and ending on a double (in Double Out games). The final score required to win the leg.
Example: "32 checkout" = D16, "40 checkout" = D20
CORK Gameplay
A throw to the bullseye to determine who throws first in a leg. Each player throws one dart at the bull - closest to center wins the cork and chooses whether to throw first or second. Also called "diddling for the middle."
Example: "Win the cork" = win the throw-off to decide who starts
D
DIDO Gameplay
Double In, Double Out - A game format where players must hit a double to start scoring AND must finish on a double. More challenging than SIDO as you need doubles at both ends of the game.
Example: Must hit D20 to start, then D16 to finish on 32
F
FILL-IN Structure
A substitute player who fills in for an absent roster player. Fill-ins allow teams to play at full strength even when regular players can't attend.
Example: "Sarah is filling in for John tonight"
L
LEG Structure
A single game within a match. One complete game of 501, Cricket, or other format from start to finish. Multiple legs often make up a set.
Example: "Best of 3 legs" = first to win 2 legs wins the set
LEVEL Structure
A player's skill rating used for balanced team formation. Levels are:
A - Advanced/high skill
B - Intermediate/mid skill
C - Developing/newer player
Example: Triples teams have one A, one B, and one C player
M
MATCH Structure
A complete contest between two teams, consisting of multiple sets. A match typically represents one night of play where teams compete for a win in the standings.
Example: "Match night is every Tuesday"
MASTER OUT Gameplay
A game format where players can finish on either a double OR a triple. This gives more checkout options compared to standard Double Out rules.
Example: Can finish 45 with T15 instead of needing a double
MPR Stats
Marks Per Round - The average number of marks scored per 3 darts (one turn) in Cricket games. A key measure of Cricket efficiency. 3.0 MPR means averaging 9 marks per turn (the maximum).
Example: An MPR of 2.5 is considered strong league play
S
SET Structure
A group of legs played together, typically between the same players. In many formats, a set is won by winning a majority of legs (e.g., best of 3, first to 2 wins).
Example: "Won the set 2-1" = won 2 legs to opponent's 1
SIDO Gameplay
Single In, Double Out - The most common game format. Players can start scoring immediately (single in) but must finish on a double to win the leg.
Example: First dart starts scoring, last dart must hit a double
