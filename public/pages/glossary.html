Averages YOUR STATS

The two headline numbers that follow you across the league.

3-Dart Average 3DA
Your average score per three darts thrown in X01 (501/301). Total points scored ÷ (darts thrown ÷ 3). A casual league player lands roughly 40–55; 60+ is strong; pros run 90+. It correctly accounts for checkout darts — if you finish on your 2nd dart, that turn counts as 2 darts, not 3.
Marks Per Round MPR
The cricket equivalent of 3DA: average marks scored per three darts. A mark = one hit on a cricket number (a single = 1 mark, double = 2, triple = 3). 2.0 is a solid league MPR; 3.0+ is excellent (you're averaging a triple every round).

Match Structure HOW A NIGHT WORKS

Three nested layers — get these and the scoreboard reads itself.

Leg
A single game — one 501 or one cricket, start to finish. The smallest unit.
Set
A grouping of legs between the same player(s), usually best-of-3. Win 2 legs, win the set.
Match
The full night between two teams — a fixed sequence of sets (the Triples League runs 9 sets a night: a mix of 501 doubles/singles and cricket singles).
Reading the score
A line like (15)7-2(8) means: home won 7 sets (with 15 total legs), away won 2 sets (with 8 total legs). The big number is sets (the match score); the numbers in parentheses are total legs (the tiebreaker depth).

X01 (501 & 301) THE COUNTDOWN GAME

Start at a number, subtract every dart, race to exactly zero.

501 / 301 / 701
The starting score. League singles/doubles are usually 501. First to land on exactly zero wins the leg.
Double-Out DO
The standard finish rule: your last dart must land in a double (the outer ring, or the bullseye which counts as a double 25). Go below 2, or hit zero without a double, and you bust (the turn doesn't count).
Double-In DI
You can't start scoring until your first scoring dart is a double. Used in DIDO formats; most league play is "straight in."
Master Out
You may finish on a double OR a triple (not just a double). A looser finish than Double-Out.
Straight (Free) In / Out
No double required to start ("straight in") or to finish ("straight out") — any segment that hits zero wins.
Bust
A turn that overshoots zero, leaves exactly 1, or hits zero without the required double. The score reverts to what it was at the start of that turn.

Checkouts FINISHING

Your remaining score when you go out is your "checkout." We track them in bands:

RangeNameWhat it takes
60–99Standard outThe bread-and-butter finishing range.
100–139Ton outA 100+ checkout — real skill.
140–160Ton-40+ outHigh, but achievable without the bull.
161+Big outRequires hitting the bullseye. Only four exist: 161, 164, 167, 170 (170 = T20-T20-Bull, the max). Tracked separately because it's a different skill.
Checkout %
How often you finish when you have a shot at a double. Successful checkouts ÷ checkout attempts (every visit where your remaining score was a finishable double).

Cricket THE TACTICAL GAME

Close out 20 through 15 plus the bull — by hitting each three times before your opponent.

Marks
Hits on a cricket number: single = 1 mark, double = 2, triple = 3. Three marks "closes" a number; further marks score points until the opponent closes it too.
Closeout
The quality of the round that finishes the game, measured in marks. Tracked 5M–9M — a 9-mark closeout (three triples) is a perfect round; 5M still wins but with less margin.
Bulls
The center counts in cricket: the outer bull (25) is a single mark, the inner bull (50) is a double mark. "3B" / "5B" on a report = a notable bull-heavy round.

Standings & Tiebreakers THE LADDER

When records are close, the league breaks ties in this exact order:

1 · Match wins
Nights won — the primary standing.
2 · Set wins
Total games (sets) won across the season, e.g. a 7-2 night.
3 · Leg wins
Individual legs won within sets — the finest scoring tier.
4 · Head-to-head
If still level, who beat whom directly decides it.

Notable Throws THE HIGHLIGHTS

The flashy stuff the scoreboard lights up in gold.

180 MAX
The maximum — three triple-20s in one turn. The biggest cheer in darts.
Ton / Ton-40+
A ton is 100+ in a turn; ton-40 is 140, and so on up to 179. Anything 100+ is a "ton."
9-Mark
The cricket max — three triples in one round (9 marks). The cricket version of a 180.
Cork
The throw at the bull to decide who goes first. Closest to the center "wins the cork" and usually chooses to throw first.
